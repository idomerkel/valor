The Bridge, February Night
River at dusk
The rescue team

It was past 10pm and the Riverside Bridge was mostly empty. I was heading home after a long shift, headphones in, thinking about nothing in particular — the way you do when your body is on autopilot.

Then I heard it. A sound below the usual city noise. Something between a gasp and a cry. I pulled out my headphones and looked over the railing, and that's when I saw him — a man, maybe sixty, caught in the current near one of the support pillars. He wasn't swimming. He was clinging.

"There was no decision. My coat was already off before my brain caught up to what my body was doing."

The water was 4°C. I know this now. At the time, the cold was just a fact — like the darkness, like the current. I reached him in about forty seconds and somehow got my arm around his chest. We both got pulled twenty meters downstream before I found the bank.

The man's name is Leonard. He sends me a card every February. This year he wrote: "You gave me five more years. My granddaughter was born last month."

I still don't know if I was brave. I think bravery might be something you only see in other people, looking back. In the moment, there was just a man in the water and a direction to go.
